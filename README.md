The EBR role is where those two things meet: structured pipeline development, customer engagement, and contributing to how organisations adopt tools that genuinely change how they build.

The conversation I had with Sam at MCP Connect Amsterdam about the bootstrapping problem in agentic tooling reinforced exactly what draws me to GitHub: the depth of thinking behind the platform and the people building it. It also made me excited about the prospect of geeking out over that kind of thing with my future colleagues during lunch or coffee breaks.

I see GitHub as a gateway towards enabling breakthroughs and innovations at scale, and at its core is something that deeply resonates with me: as the saying goes, if you want to go fast, go alone; if you want to go far, go together. The fact that developers can build on each other's work, comment, contribute, and collaborate openly is exactly the kind of culture I want to be part of and contribute to.

## How I approach enterprise accounts in the Dutch and EMEA market

I am embedded in the Dutch and European AI and developer ecosystem: active at events like MCP Connect, connected to founders scaling engineering teams, and in regular contact with VCs and corporate innovation leads across the Netherlands and EMEA. This gives me warm access to the exact stakeholders GitHub needs to reach, and the ability to open conversations that do not start with a cold email.

When I identify a new account, my approach is:

1. Research the engineering footprint, recent hires, and public statements on tooling and developer experience
2. Identify the warmest entry point through my network or event presence
3. Lead with a relevant insight tied to their specific context, not a product pitch
4. Qualify against pain, timeline, and decision-making structure before involving the sales team
5. Contribute to the account plan and maintain the relationship through the cycle

The stakeholders I map from day one:

| Stakeholder | Role in the deal | What they care about |
|---|---|---|
| VP / Head of Engineering | Champion | Developer productivity, tooling consistency, reducing friction |
| Director of Platform Engineering / DevEx | Technical champion | Developer experience, standardisation, tooling efficiency |
| CISO / Head of Security | Influencer / blocker | Supply chain security, secret scanning, compliance |
| CTO | Economic buyer / executive sponsor | Competitive velocity, engineering culture, ROI |
| Team leads | End users | Daily workflow, adoption ease, AI assistance |
| Finance / Procurement | Gatekeeper | Cost control, contract structure, risk mitigation |
